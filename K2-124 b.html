K2-124 b

K2-124 b K2-124 b is a hot super-Earth exoplanet orbiting the star K2-124. It's one of the first exoplanets discovered by NASA's Kepler Space Telescope.


Key characteristics
  • Type: Super-Earth

  • Transiting: K2-124 b passes in front of its star as seen from Earth; a phenomenon known as "transiting." This allows astronomers to measure its size and atmospheric properties.

  • Orbital period: 1.5 days

  • Temperature: K2-124 b orbits extremely close to its star, making it incredibly hot. Its surface temperature is estimated to be thousands of degrees Fahrenheit.


Significance of its discovery


  • Understanding exoplanets: K2-124 b has been a valuable target for studying exoplanets. Its extreme conditions provide insights into the diversity of planetary systems beyond our own.

  • Atmospheric studies: Scientists have attempted to study the atmosphere of K2-124 b to learn more about its composition and potential for habitability. However, due to its extreme heat, it's unlikely to be habitable.
